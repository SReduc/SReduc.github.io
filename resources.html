Resources for Class Practices and Projects

Here is an overview of Plickers, which we will use as a way to quickly answer and discuss class quizes.

Throughout the year we will be doing citizen science projects. Here are some websites to find a project that interests you: National Geographic, CitizenScience.org, Zooniverse, and SciStarter.

We will also be doing quite a bit of online research. Here are some databases and search engines for easy access:
Google Scholar works like a basic Google search, but returns academic articles and books. Search results will also show if there is a free pdf of the article on the author's website or in a database.
Pubmed is the NIH database of articles on medicine, microbiology, and biotechnology.
The NIH also has a database for Looking up sequenced genomes and a program for comparing gene or protein sequences (BLAST).
EBSCO is one of the largest academic databases for both online and print information. Our school has limited access to full-text articles, but this is a good starting place for looking at abstracts and learning jargon.
If you are stuck, here is UNM's Guide to research and scholarly articles and Guide to reading original research.
